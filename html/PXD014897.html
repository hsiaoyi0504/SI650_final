Dataset Summary
TitleSalvage pathway maintains functional respiratory complex I
DescriptionRegulation of the turnover of complex I (CI), the largest mitochondrial respiratory chain complex remains enigmatic, despite huge advancement in understanding its structure and the assembly steps in recent years. Although some studies indicated that CI might be remodelled in response to various stimuli, the mechanism mediating these putative changes is unknown. Here, we report that the NADH-oxidising N-module which sits on the tip of the CI peripheral arm is turned over at a higher rate and largely independently of the rest of the complex. The N-module turnover is mediated by mitochondrial matrix protease ClpXP, which selectively removes and degrades damaged core subunits. The observed mechanism seems to be a safeguard against accumulation of dysfunctional CI arising from inactivation of the N-module subunits due to attrition caused by extensive redox activity. This CI salvage pathway maintains highly functional CI and healthy mitochondria through an energetically favourable mechanism that demands much lower energetic cost than de novo synthesis and reassembly of the entire CI.
