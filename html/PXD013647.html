Dataset Summary
TitleThe tumor suppressor SCRIB is a negative modulator of the Wnt/-catenin signaling (part 1)
DescriptionEpithelial tissues are highly organized structures that rely on cell polarity pathways driven by membrane receptors and scaffolding proteins. SCRIBBLE is a cytoplasmic scaffold present at cell-cell junctions in polarized cells that contains LRR and PDZ domains as its paralogue LANO, a member of the LAP protein family. The family is composed of DENSIN-180, ERBIN, SCRIB and LRRC1. Based on phylogenic tree, LAP proteins can be split in two branches with the first branch is made up with SCRIB and LRCC1 and the second one of ERBIN and DENSIN-180. The current interactome of SCRIB and LRRC1 consists of 67 and 30 protein interaction partners respectively with only 9 (10%) common partners (BioGRID, version 3.5). Our study uncovers the proteome associated to SCRIB and LRRC1 and described for the first time protein associated to each paralogue.
